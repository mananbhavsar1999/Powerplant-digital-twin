Fire-Tube Boiler

Sizing and heat-transfer basis for the steam generator supplying the 100-house plant model.

01 Why a fire-tube boiler

At this scale — roughly 472 kW of thermal duty and 636 kg/hr of steam — a fire-tube (shell) boiler is the standard industrial choice. Hot combustion gas passes through a bundle of small tubes submerged in a water-filled shell, transferring heat to the water around them. Water-tube boilers, where the arrangement is reversed, are reserved for larger, higher-pressure utility-scale plants where their higher capital cost is justified. Below a few tons of steam per hour, fire-tube construction is simpler, cheaper, and the realistic engineering choice.

This boiler sits within a simple condensing Rankine cycle: Boiler → Turbine → Condenser → Feedwater Pump → back to Boiler. No compressor is used anywhere in the plant — that component belongs to gas turbine cycles. The feedwater pump is the liquid-side equivalent, re-pressurizing condensate back to boiler pressure.

03 Required heat transfer area

The boiler shell must provide enough tube surface area to transfer 471.6 kW from combustion gas into the surrounding water. Using a conservative heat flux assumption typical of small fire-tube boilers:

Heat Flux Assumption

q = 25 kW/m²  — conservative design value for small shell boilers

Required Area = Boiler Duty ÷ Heat Flux
Required Area = 471.6 kW ÷ 25 kW/m²
18.9 m² of tube surface area required

04 Fire-tube count

Assuming standard small fire-tube dimensions — 50 mm outer diameter, 2.2 m length (matching the boiler shell) — the surface area per tube and the resulting tube count are:

Per-Tube Surface Area
A_tube = π × OD × Length
A_tube = π × 0.05 m × 2.2 m
0.346 m² per tube
Tube Count
Tube Count = Required Area ÷ A_tube
Tube Count = 18.9 m² ÷ 0.346 m²
≈ 55 fire-tubes
This is a first-order estimate sized for the 3D model's tube bundle density, not a certified pressure-vessel design. A real boiler design would additionally account for gas-side velocity, fouling factors, draft loss, and manufacturer-specific performance data.
